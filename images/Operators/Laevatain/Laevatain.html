"Let that twilight burn!"
Laevatain is a Rhodes island operator who joined Endfield at the Endministrator's direct recommendation. Under the framework
agreement between the two companies, she serves as an operator directly under the Endiminstrator's command.
Before joining Endfield Industries, Laevatain raomed the world alone, pursuing a goal she could call her own. Because of that
journey, and her formidable skill in solo combat, people often mistake her for "someone you can't talk to." In truth, the opposite is
closer to reality. It's only that her earnest, fortright nature sometimes comes across too strongly, giving rise to misunderstanding.
At times, though, she speaks in ways that confound others, often circling back to the subject of "memory", as if she were purposefully
trying to separate herself from who she is. She often wields flames, yet craves ice cream just as much. To her, maybe that contradiction feels perfectly natural.
"Try getting closer to me if you're still feeling cold."

Reconvener and Operator Laevatain joined Endfield with the Endministrator's direct recommendation. Laevatain arrived at the reception area a full 45 minutes ahead of the agreed-upon schedule. She simply placed the employment notification on the table and said little else except, "I'm here. We can begin." She was quite cooperative during the initial phase of the interview — giving her signature, submitting a retinal scan, and linking her Protocol Authority Level without a single fuss or comment. I tried to liven up the atmosphere by saying, "Welcome to Endfield." She nodded and replied, "I'm already here." During job-inclination testing, I followed the prescribed list of questions and asked if she considered utilizing her skill set in other non-combat roles, such as combat planner, coach, or instructor. She did not say anything but appeared obviously annoyed. In the end, Operator Laevatain left with this final line: "Just let me go to as many places you can — places that let me build as much memories as I can." — Martin Marvin Malen, Assistant, HR Division, Endfield Industries

FILE 1

Endministrator and Supervisor Perlica of Endfield Industries:
I am extremely pleased to learn that Laevatain has returned with the Endministrator's help. Though her journey is far from complete, her return proves that she has found a new objective and people whom she is willing to travel with.
Extremely powerful "desire and hope" drove Laevatain to step onto the lands of Talos-II. When we first sensed her existence, the light she gave off was bedazzling. I later realized that this light was emitted by her obsession — her innermost ego demanding the answer: "Who am I?"
Led on by the said obsession, the fragments awakened and created a complete persona. "She" walked out of the Originium Woods, new and intact, and arrived at Rhodes Island bearing the name "Laevatain". I thought she would immediately become one of our strongest assets and supporters, but she turned us down. Just like Surtr who had always walked alone, no one can command Laevatain or force her to stay in one place. She will answer to no faction until she finds the answer to dispel her own miasma of amnesia. Hence, we decided to open the doors and give her our best farewells.
Once, I was worried that Laevatain's choice would affect other reconveners. Then I realized that they became reconveners because every one of them did not forget the old dream and vision. And that is why they reconvened with you on this very same path. Now, this thought alone has lifted a stone off my chest.
Since Laevatain is currently an official Endfielder fighting along your side, then allow me to share a few words of advice (based on my knowledge of "her") for "getting along with her".
Firstly, accommodate and accept Laevatain's straightforward nature. She often says whatever that comes to her mind. It is not an act of hostility, malice, or rudeness. In fact, such words are a symbol of her sincere and undisguised attitude — "I prefer to simply tell you what I'm thinking right now."
Secondly, should Laevatain exhibit obvious signs of fury or impatience, do not attempt to persuade her to "calm" down. Believe me, there are more effective means, such as the provision of ice cream or other things that can rapidly "cool" the situation. Laevatain is someone you can communicate with, but she dislikes "being told what to do".
Just as a side note, before she left Rhodes Island, she took all the crimsonberry ice cream from my lab freezer — and ate them all, right before my eyes, as though it was the right thing to do. "Thought you told me memories can lie," she said as she licked the spoon, "So I better have more of this to make it as memorable as possible." Laevatain marked her farewell with this action to ensure the veracity of the moment. She actually cares very deeply about her friends, far more deeply than most would like to think.
Thirdly, Laevatain is extremely similar to Surtr, but according to her temperament... Do not tell her that she resembles Surtr. Also, do not tell her that she DOES NOT resemble Surtr. In fact, do not ever compare her to Surtr, unless she brings the topic up herself. Always remember this!
Best wishes in your collective journey across Talos-II.

Rhodes Island

DOCUMENT: Endfield Industries Medical Division Service Log
AUTHOR: Dulla Gray, clinical psychologist

LOG 1
Surgeon Answorth has been dispatched to the surface in response to an emergency. So, this is my first time providing psychotherapeutic services on my own ever since I joined Endfield. The details of the job is to provide psychological evaluations for an onboarding operator. I hope this is the start of a beautiful relationship between us.
............
I've waited for six hours but the operator did not show up.

LOG 2
I verified the records. Turns out the operator is a reconvener troubled by "polymnestic disorders". I remain the chief therapist in charge of this case.
My second meeting (which is technically the first, all facts considered) with Operator Laevatain was successful. I did the psychological evaluations that we missed last time, and she also explained why she failed to show up for that meeting. It appeared that she was deeply troubled and annoyed by certain nightmares. Hence, she chose to head to the surface and "sort the feelings out". With my encouragement, she agreed to describe her dreams to me.

Laevatain dreamed of herself standing in a classroom. One by one her classmates introduced themselves on the stage. But when it was her turn, she tried to open her mouth but no voice came out. She tried again, but her throat began to hurt and she only managed to emit blurred, raspy noises.
The teacher turned around, but a raging flame danced where her face should be, "You could not say it because you never had your own life."
Laevatain tried to leave the classroom, only to find herself in a familiar yet alien-looking house. Everything around her was burning. The fire consumed the walls, the books, and the mirrors. Her classmates, friends, teachers, families, and even colleagues she met in Rhodes Island and Dijiang stood in the room, but all their faces were contortions of twisted flame and no longer recognizable.
She then ran for the door and forced her way through, only to find herself in the exact same house, also on fire. She ran again, opened the door, and saw the same thing. With every door she went through, the room got smaller and the flames got closer. In the end, she opened the final door and arrived at a room of ashes with neither fire nor smoke. She saw herself seated in the room, smiling at her:
"You finally burnt everything."

Laevatain's recurring dream appears to be rooted in "memories" she has carried in her mind. Whether these are drawn from real experiences or mere illusions is unclear, but it seems she has been journeying with this burden for a long time.

LOG 3
I tried prescribing several recommended therapies for Operator Laevatain but the results were less than ideal. After receiving suggestions from Surgeon Answorth, I asked Laevatain if she ever considered medical removal of these memories as the scenes and nightmares continue to affect her in an unabated fashion.
She pondered for a moment and denied this advice. Several days later, she described another dream to me. In this dream, Laevatain woke up in a white world. The sky was clean and flawless, and the land seemed endless.
She walked for a long time without feeling the wind. She met no one, heard nothing, and saw no shadows.
A door stood at the very end. Posted on the door was a note — Enter, if you do not wish to be anyone else.
There she stopped. She chose not to open the door.

When the session ended, Operator Laevatain gave me a bunch of fiery red flowers — a token of gratitude for "future sessions of memory-sharing".

FILE 4 (Have 200% Trust with Laevatain)

Regular Work Report for Operators Under the Endministrator
REPORTED BY: Laevatain

I escorted the messenger to this volcanic basin. Molten lava flowed through the ground beneath our feet, heating the rain and creek, causing the earth to froth and bubble endlessly. The monitoring station stood right atop this "cauldron of living soup".
This place is extremely poor in Originium. Resident scientists rely on geothermal heat to cook food. A ration tin can be cooked through simply by burying it in the dirt for an hour. They even have "pocket stews": a liquid stock and other ingredients sealed in a food-grade bag — after quick cooking, you can just tear it open with your teeth. I got a face full of hot stew once, and it felt like I was on fire.
When I had my first meal, the scientist taught me to sing their "ritual" chant: "May your soup never spill, and may your flame never die."
I sang the chant with him. It was the first time I received a stranger's blessing on Talos-II.
— Sent from Monitoring Station 34

As the Operator closest to the incident and the most capable emergency responder, I was ordered to reach the UWST base not marked on the map with the greatest speed I could muster. The issue was not complicated. Just an unexpected blast furnace malfunction, shutdown, and loss of power. I spent a bit of my powers and helped them re-ignite the core. Temperature returned to operational levels and the system was restored. The crisis was temporarily averted.
Their "names" left quite an impression on me. The place was a secret base in the frigid lands of █████, usually referred to by its unique beacon code. Everyone there was forbidden from using their real names or any self-chosen codenames; only the "assigned" name was allowed.
My temporary name here was "Olga Randall". I was told there were three others before me with the same name, and it's likely that the next replacement actuary sent here will also take on this name.
It was a strange experience. I was just a temporary user of the name "Olga Randall". It felt similar to the memory fragments swimming in my mind. Neither the name nor the fragments were truly mine, but at that very moment, they were all part of me.
— Sent from UWST Beacon DAU.14

After eliminating the Aggelos camping in the freight station, I reopened the gate that had kept the tunnel sealed for quite a while.
They maintained a low gravity environment within the tunnels. Refugees, trapped here for months, floated their way to the exit, trembling and struggling as they left the place. They must relearn how to walk and run under the ponderous influence of gravity.
But there was a little girl who floated within the tunnels and danced for a bit before leaving the place. She told me it was how she kept herself calm and sane when they were trapped in this place.
I memorized her dance, a comforting yet powerful routine of rhythms. If you want, I can show it to you and let you have a go once I head back. The moves aren't hard. It's all about the breathing and the rhythms.
— Sent from freight nexus MC-20

I've made plenty of new memories during this time. I still remember that you're chasing after your own memories as well. I found a few places related to the old "you". Seek me out if you're interested.
— Emergency message, sent from Contact Point 084A
